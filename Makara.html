Makara Wear

From "Brochure" to Conversion: Optimizing the E-Commerce Flow for Sustainable Luxury

Role

UI/UX Designer

Timeline

2024 September

Tools

Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, Slack

The Challenge: High Interest, High Friction

Makara Wear offers sustainable, comfort-focused swimwear, but their digital experience wasn't closing the deal. The original Product Detail Page was cluttered and outdated, acting as a bottleneck to revenue.

  • The "Invisible" CTA: The "Add to Cart" button was small and monochromatic, blending into the background.
  • Payment Friction: There was no quick-checkout option (PayPal), forcing users through a long form entry—a major drop-off point for mobile shoppers.
  • Missed Revenue: Users had no easy way to "match" items (e.g., buying the bottom that matches the top), resulting in lower Average Order Value (AOV).

The Solution: Designing for Confidence

I redesigned the PDP to align with the brand’s "Sustainable Luxury" identity while aggressively removing purchase friction.

Visual Clarity & Hierarchy

  • Immersive Media: I replaced small thumbnails with a high-resolution, editorial-style gallery and integrated video. This allows users to feel the fabric comfort visually, reducing "fit anxiety."
  • The "Green Light" Strategy: I introduced a high-contrast Green CTA ("Add to Cart") paired with a distinct Yellow PayPal button. This visual separation guides the eye immediately to the conversion action.

The Monetization Move (Cross-Selling)

Complete the Look: I implemented a dynamic "You May Also Like" section directly below the fold. This solves the user's problem ("What matches this?") and solves the business problem (increasing cart size).

Mobile-First Optimization

Recognizing that fashion traffic is 80%+ mobile, I redesigned the mobile view to mimic social media feeds—full-width vertical scrolling images that feel native and intuitive. I reorganized the size selection and product info into clean dropdowns, ensuring the "Buy" buttons remain the primary focus on small screens.

The Result

The redesign transformed the page from a static display into a high-converting funnel. By adding alternative payment methods (PayPal) and simplifying the visual hierarchy, we removed the barriers between "wanting" and "buying."

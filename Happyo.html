Happyo

Converting Skepticism into Trust: A Science-First Approach to Weight Loss

Role

UI/UX Designer

Timeline

2024 December - 2025 April

Tools

Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, ChatGPT, Slack

The Challenge: The "Generic" Barrier

Happyo, a mental health and hypnotherapy app, had a web-based sales funnel that was functional but unengaging. The original design suffered from "Static Information Overload":

  • Cognitive Overload: Users were greeted with walls of text and generic stock imagery that failed to connect emotionally with their specific struggles (e.g., procrastination vs. anxiety).
  • Low Engagement: The flow felt like a standard checkout process rather than a therapeutic journey, leading to high drop-off rates before users even reached the pricing page.
  • Dated Aesthetics: The web interface lacked the polished, app-like "feel" that modern mobile users expect from a paid digital product.

The Solution: A "Listen-First" Experience

I redesigned the entire acquisition flow in Figma, shifting from a passive landing page to an active, gamified assessment.

The "Investment" Loop (The Quiz)

Instead of telling users what the app does, I designed an interactive quiz that asks them what they need.

  • Micro-Commitments: By breaking big questions into simple, tappable cards (e.g., "What is your main goal?"), I utilized the "Sunk Cost Bias." A user who has already tapped 5 times is significantly more likely to finish the process than one who just reads a headline.
  • Visual Feedback: I added progress bars and "success" animations between steps to give users small dopamine hits, keeping them motivated to finish the flow.

The "Magic" Moment (Data Visualization)

  • The "Calculating" Screen: I introduced a dedicated loading screen that says "Personalizing your plan..." This is a crucial trust-building moment (known as the Labor Illusion). It signals to the user that the app is custom-tailoring a solution just for them, justifying the price tag.
  • The Payoff: The final result isn't just a "Buy Now" button; it’s a personalized summary (e.g., "Your Procrastination Score: High"). This reframes the subscription not as a purchase, but as a necessary solution to a diagnosed problem.

Visual Modernization

  • Clean Typography & Whitespace: I stripped away the clutter, using bold, rounded typography and ample whitespace to reduce anxiety—mirroring the product's core promise of "mental clarity."
  • Consistent Branding: I unified the color palette to soft, calming gradients that feel premium and trustworthy.

The Result

The redesign transformed a static web page into a high-converting "pre-onboarding" experience. By switching to a quiz-based model, we increased the perceived value of the product, ensuring that when users finally see the price, they see it as an investment in their personalized plan rather than a cost.

Novitrim

Converting Skepticism into Trust: A Science-First Approach to Weight Loss

Role

UI/UX Designer

Timeline

2024 December - 2025 January

Tools

Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, ChatGPT, Slack

The Challenge: Breaking the "Snake Oil" Stigma

The weight loss supplement market is saturated with loud visuals that erode user trust. My client approached me with a physical product (gummies) but a distinct goal: Novitrim needed to look like medicine, not candy.

The target audience—middle-aged users focused on weight control—requires validation, not just marketing. My challenge was to design a 0-to-1 brand identity and user flow that bridged the gap between e-commerce and telemedicine.

The Solution: The "Diagnosis" Pattern

Instead of a traditional shopping cart experience, I designed a Consultative UX. I leveraged the "Labor Illusion" psychological principle—showing the user that the system is "working" for them to increase the perceived value of the result.

Visual Strategy: Clinical Warmth

To differentiate Novitrim from aggressive competitors, I created a "Natural Clinical" aesthetic:

  • Color Palette: I utilized deep earthy browns to signal "natural ingredients" anchored by sterile white space to communicate "clinical safety."
  • Authority Bias: I strategically placed "Doctor Approved" badges and medical professional profiles above the fold. In a low-trust industry, borrowing authority is the fastest way to conversion.

The "Monetization" Move

I optimized the checkout flow to reduce abandonment anxiety:

  • Trust Anchoring: I placed security badges (Norton, McAfee) and "Honest Reviews" directly adjacent to the credit card input fields.
  • Bundle Logic: The pricing cards highlight the "Best Seller" options with clear daily cost breakdowns ("Only $1.2 per day"), reframing the price as a minor daily habit rather than a large upfront cost.

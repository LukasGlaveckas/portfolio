Hypnotherapy platform

Hypnozio

From idea to 15,000,000+ users

I led the product design for Hypnozio, transforming a raw concept into a global mental health platform with 15,000,000+ users. By shifting from a content-library model to a gamified "program" architecture, and rigorously optimizing the monetization funnel, I helped scale the product into the company’s primary revenue driver.

Role

Lead UI/UX Designer

Timeline

2023 - Present

Tools

Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, ChatGPT

Context

The company offers digital hypnotherapy programs for people struggling with issues such as binge eating, insomnia, anxiety, and smoking.

The goal was to build a hypnotherapy platform that would:

  • Provide a seamless and intuitive experience for users
  • Offer a range of hypnotherapy programs for different issues
  • Allow users to track their progress and receive personalized recommendations
  • Provide a secure and private environment for users to access their programs

The Challenge: Building a Scalable Ecosystem

The business goal was ambitious: dominate the digital hypnotherapy market for mass-market issues like anxiety and insomnia. However, the MVP state was non-existent—no user flows, no checkout infrastructure, and no validation.

My Core Objectives:

  • 0 to 1 Architecture: Build a scalable system to house hundreds of audio sessions without cognitive overload.
  • CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization): Engineer a sales funnel that converted high-intent traffic into recurring subscribers.
  • Trust & Safety: Design an interface that felt clinical and safe, yet accessible to a general audience.

Strategic Pivot: The "Program" Mental Model

The insight: early testing revealed that users felt overwhelmed by a "library" of audio files. They didn't know where to start.

The solution: I restructured the content strategy from "Tracks" to "Daily Progressions."

  • Behavioral design: I utilized the Zeigarnik Effect (the human desire to complete started tasks) by breaking therapy into structured, day-by-day courses.
  • Impact: This reduced decision fatigue and increased Day-1 retention, as users felt they were on a guided journey rather than browsing a catalogue.

Mastering the Monetization Funnel

The problemt: Initial subscription conversion was below targets. The value proposition wasn't clear at the point of purchase.

The Design Intervention: I redesigned the paywall experience to maximize Average Order Value (AOV).

  • Anchoring: I design ed the pricing cards to visually anchor the "6-Month Plan" as the rational choice, highlighting the savings to drive users toward higher LTV (Lifetime Value) commitments.
  • Friction Reduction: I optimized the checkout flow by introducing the paywall after the user had invested time in the assessment quiz (Sunk Cost fallacy), but before results were shown.
  • Result: This structure stabilized our CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) and became the global standard for the app’s expansion.

Design Ops: Building for Scale

To support 15 million users across multiple languages, I moved away from ad-hoc design.

  • The System: I established an Atomic Design system (standardized typography, spacing tokens, and components) to bridge the gap between design and engineering.
  • Efficiency: This allowed us to deploy localized versions of the app rapidly without accruing design debt.

The Outcome

Hypnozio is not just a project; it is a category leader.

  • Scale: Grew from 0 to 15,000,000 users.
  • Adoption: Became the core revenue driver for the parent company.
  • Rating: Maintained a high user sentiment score despite aggressive scaling.

Key Learnings: This project taught me that "pretty UI" doesn't scale. Systems and psychology scale. The success came not just from the visual polish, but from aligning the user's desire for self-improvement with a frictionless business model.
